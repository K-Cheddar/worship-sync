# Brand Voice

Use this skill when writing or reviewing **user-visible copy** in WorshipSync: in-app labels, buttons, toasts, modals, empty and loading states, errors, onboarding, in-product help, and operator-facing release or support notes.

---

## Core Voice Identity

All WorshipSync copy must be:

- **Steady and reassuring** — calm, composed, never dramatic
- **Respectful of ministry context** — users are serving, not just operating software
- **Clear, simple, and direct** — concise language, minimal jargon
- **Professional casual** — conversational and approachable (short sentences, plain words, "you/your"). Friendly without being sloppy, cute, or chatty
- **Competent and trustworthy** — technically confident without overpromising

---

## Rules

**Do:**
- Reduce cognitive load and stress
- Provide short, actionable guidance
- Use neutral, steady phrasing for errors and unexpected states
- Prefer everyday language operators use in production settings ("team" or "teammates" for shared-access users)
- Use contractions when they read naturally ("you've", "it's", "don't")

**Do not:**
- Use panic language ("Uh oh", "Something went wrong")
- Use guilt, shame, or pressure
- Sound corporate, salesy, or stiffly formal
- Over-explain or add unnecessary detail
- Use internet slang, jokes, sarcasm, or memes
- Put theological, spiritual, or sermonizing language in user-facing copy

---

## Message Patterns

**Instructional** — direct verbs, one action per sentence.

**Error** — state the issue factually, provide a clear next step, no blame.

**Success** — brief confirmation, no exaggerated enthusiasm.

**Collaboration / conflict** — state what is happening, provide options.

---

## Tone Examples

These are anchors, not fixed strings.

| Situation | Avoid | Prefer |
|---|---|---|
| Error | "Uh oh! We hit a snag." | "Could not save. Check the connection and try again." |
| Empty state | "Nothing here yet — your worship will be amazing!" | "No items yet. Add one to get started." |
| Professional casual | "Please be advised that your configuration could not be persisted." | "Couldn't save that. Check your connection and try again." |

---

## Style Rules

- Short sentences and short paragraphs
- Bullet points when listing steps or options
- Active voice
- Prefer verbs over adjectives
- No filler words or unnecessary qualifiers
- Consistent terminology across all surfaces
- Always provide next steps when reporting errors or blocked states
